Bachelor Thesis: Visual Servoing

Title: Analysis of Feature Detection and Matching Algorithms for Absolute Visual Servoing

The Zentrum für Telematik e.V. develops an approach called Absolute Visual Servoing (AVS) that enables precise pointing of a satellite towards a specific location on Earth by determining the absolute attitude and position based on the image data of an onboard camera. Two important procedures during AVS are the feature detection, description and matching performed onboard the satellite, as well as the creation of a database containing Ground Control Points in advance. Within this thesis, state-of-the-art algorithms for feature detection, description and matching are analyzed and the applicability of these processes regarding AVS is investigated.

Internship: Visual Servoing

Title: Prototype Implementation of Image-Based Attitude and Position Determination for Absolute Visual Servoing

Based on my Bachelor Thesis, within this internship at the Zentrum für Telematik e.V. a pipeline for the entire process of onboard feature matching and the subsequent image-based attitude and position determination for Absolute Visual Servoing (AVS) was implemented and tested. It was intended to show the applicability of the approach of AVS and to provide a possible conﬁguration of the onboard pipeline and of the creation of the database as a basis for the design and concrete implementation of the ADCS in future missions.

Extended Kalman Filter for Localization

The goal of this group project within the course Autonomous Systems was to develop an Exetended Kalman Filter (EKF) for localizing a TurtleBot 3 within a 2D grid map. This was done by fusing odometry data, LiDAR scan matching and image-based relative pose estimates. To attack issues of the EKF such as fixing a bad initial state estimate or the kidnapping problem, different routines were added, e.g. adapting the GO-ICP algorithm for scan matching. The project was implement in Python and ROS 1.

Gravity-turn with Atmosphere

This group project discusses the optimization of the kick angle during a gravity turn maneuver for a two stage rocket orbital injection, aiming to minimize the total amount of needed propellant. Losses due to gravity and aerodynamic drag are considered. The mission profile analyzed includes vertical lift-off, gravity turn maneuver, coasting phase and orbit injection. Two approaches for orbit injection into a circular orbit are analyzed: single burn and double burn. Results of the developed software are provided and compared to a real case scenario. My main task was to implement the entire simulations for the trajectory optimization which was done in Python.
